Marie Curie

Click to learn more

Marie Curie (1867-1934)

Physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields, and the first woman to become a professor at the University of Paris.

Nobel Prize in Physics (1903) Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1911)
"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood."

Rosa Parks

Click to learn more

Rosa Parks (1913-2005)

American activist in the civil rights movement best known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery bus boycott. Her refusal to give up her seat to a white passenger on a segregated bus became an important symbol of the movement against racial segregation.

Presidential Medal of Freedom Congressional Gold Medal
"I have learned over the years that when one's mind is made up, this diminishes fear."

Frida Kahlo

Click to learn more

Frida Kahlo (1907-1954)

Mexican painter known for her many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by nature and artifacts of Mexico. Her art has been celebrated for its uncompromising depiction of the female form and experience, and its frank depiction of women's traditional roles.

First Mexican artist featured at the Louvre Cultural icon
"I paint self-portraits because I am so often alone, because I am the person I know best."

Malala Yousafzai

Click to learn more

Malala Yousafzai (1997-Present)

Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She is known for human rights advocacy, especially the education of women and children in her native Pakistan, where the Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school.

Nobel Peace Prize (2014) UN Messenger of Peace
"One child, one teacher, one book, one pen can change the world."
